What is your company going to make? Please describe your product and what it does or will do.
We are building an AI-native concierge that plans a user's entire social outing, from discovery to booking. A user can ask in natural language—like 'Find me a trendy patio with good cocktails for two tonight'—and our agent will find hidden gems by triangulating Google Maps, trending Tiktok/Instragram content, Reddit, and other sources across the web. Critically, our agent then makes automated, outbound phone calls to venues to handle the "last mile" that trips up all other apps. It can confirm real-time details ('Is your patio open?'), check for walk-in availability, and make reservations at places that aren't on Resy or OpenTable, automating the entire fragmented planning process into a single step. It automates the fragmented process of piecing together data from multiple apps and manually calling to confirm its assumptions.

Explain your decision regarding location.
Our ideal customers tell us that while New York has a sea of options, existing providers fail at finding locations that meet complex, specific needs. For example, when a user searches for 'Chinese Halal Food' Google just pulls up 'halal carts'. This market is perfect for someone who needs more complex tools due to an abundance of options, and allows us to stay as close to our customers as possible.

Why did you pick this idea to work on? Do you have domain expertise in this area? How do you know people need what you're making?
Even with an abundance of options, finding activities in New York can be a pain, but depending on who we are with and our circumstances each time, there might be a variety of constraints. Trying to navigate time/diet/vibe restrictions based on traditional tools is time-consuming. We wanted something we could use ourselves that was better. We interviewed over sixty friends and family casually throughout the weeks, and formally interviewed forty people in addition. A clear trend emerged that we are not alone. People want a less time-consuming way to find places and activities that work for them.
Who are your competitors? What do you understand about your business that they don't?
Who are your competitors? Our competitors are fragmented across the user's planning process: For Discovery: TikTok and Instagram For Search/Reviews: Google Maps and Yelp For Booking: Resy and OpenTable There is also a direct consumer app called Corner, which has great branding but no clear AI strategy to solve the "cold start" problem for new users. What do you understand that they don't? We understand that the entire real-world planning process is a broken, multi-app nightmare. A user discovers a "hidden gem" on TikTok, vets it on Google Maps, checks the vibe on Instagram, and then hits a dead end because the venue is secretly closed and Google's static database was never updated. Our competitors don't understand this because they are all solving just one piece of the problem. They are utilities, not agents. TikTok is entertainment, not a utility. Google is built on a static database. Neither can bridge the "last mile" to confirm real-time, unstructured data—like, "Is your patio open tonight?" or "Do you have space for two in 30 minutes?" We solve this by being a full-stack agent. Our AI aggregates trending content and then makes outbound calls to execute the user's request, automating that entire fragmented process into a single step.
