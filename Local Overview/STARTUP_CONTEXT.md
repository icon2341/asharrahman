# Local - Startup Context for AI Agents

> **Purpose:** This document provides comprehensive context about "Local" to assist future AI agents in understanding the company's product, positioning, target audience, brand voice, and strategic roadmap.

## 1. Startup Overview

*   **Name:** Local
*   **Product:** An AI-native personal concierge platform / agent that plans and books IRL experiences. It transforms the fragmented 5-app social planning process (TikTok -> Google -> Maps -> Resy/Instagram) into a unified, automated workflow. The agent uses search, proprietary data, and multimodal AI, but its defining feature is executing the "last mile": it makes automated outbound phone calls to venues to verify perishable, unstructured data (e.g., walk-in wait times, patio availability). 
*   **Founders:** Ashar Rahman (CEO) & Liam Stamper (CTO) – formerly founding engineers at Aviary AI (YC S22).
*   **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):** 
    *   Power users of social planning (frequent diners, weekend planners).
    *   Transplants (like the founders who moved to NYC) overwhelmed by an abundance of options and specific constraints.
    *   People frustrated by the unpaid labor of validating discovery content (vetting TikTok trends against Google reviews and calling for availability).
*   **Category:** AI Concierge / Consumer App / Search & Booking
*   **Key Moats:** 
    *   Proprietary dataset of 30,000 NYC venues with "essence" data (vibe, dietary, noise, crowd) built by bypassing Google Places using AI scraping.
    *   Voice agent infrastructure that handles the execution layer, capturing data no API provides.
    *   Vertical integration (Discovery → Verification → Booking).

## 2. Content Pillars (3-5)

These are the core mental models and themes for output creation:

1.  **The "Last Mile" of Planning & Customer Pain:** Highlight the broken, multi-app nightmare of current social planning. Emphasize that planning is a *labor/workflow* problem, not just a *search* problem. APIs provide static data; Local provides *execution*.
2.  **Autonomous Execution (Behind-the-Scenes):** Showcase the AI voice agents in action. Demonstrate how the concierge makes actual outbound phone calls to check real-time availability and confirm details, replacing the "unpaid labor" usually pushed onto the user.
3.  **Data Moat & Deep Utility (Product Tips):** Focus on the specific utility features:
    *   The "Rolodex": Saving URLs (like IG Reels) which the AI processes (download, summarize, vector embed) for semantic search later.
    *   The proprietary AI-generated database that knows a venue's "vibe" rather than just its basic location categories.
4.  **Founder Story & Extreme Velocity:** Anchor the narrative in the founders' experience as NYC transplants solving their own hyper-specific planning problems. Highlight the elite, AI-native engineering velocity (e.g., building 90% of an iOS app using autonomous coding agents like Claude Code and Linear MCP).

## 3. Brand Voice & Do-Not-Do List

### Brand Voice Notes
*   **Confident & Execution-Oriented:** "We don't just search—we execute."
*   **Technically Sophisticated but Consumer-Accessible:** Explain complex AI (RAG, multimodal embeddings, call orchestration) in terms of the magic it delivers to the end-user (e.g., "AI food bloggers," "Chat with your Rolodex").
*   **Fast-Moving & Disruptive:** Tone should reflect extreme velocity, building technical moats, and bypassing lumbering incumbents (Google Places, Yelp).

### Do-Not-Do List (Topics/Angles to Avoid)
*   **DO NOT frame as just a search or discovery app:** Avoid direct comparisons to Google, Perplexity, Yelp, or TikTok purely on the axis of "finding things." The differentiator is *doing the work* after finding the thing.
*   **DO NOT rely on "Cold Start" social features:** Avoid positioning the product like Beli or Corner where the user must build out a social graph to get value. The AI does the work, not the user's friends.
*   **DO NOT use generic AI buzzwords without utility:** Always tie AI capabilities back to the specific problem they solve (e.g., don't just say "multimodal AI", say "multimodal AI to instantly extract the venue from a viral TikTok and save it to your Rolodex").

## 4. Geography + Language

*   **Geography:** United States (Initial hyper-focus on New York City, with roadmap expansion to LA, Miami, SF).
*   **Language:** English.

## 5. Strategic Roadmap To Monetization (Path to $1k MRR)

1.  **Phase 1: The Ultimate Planner / Beli 2.0:** 
    *   Launch an app featuring intelligent, AI-powered search that surpasses the existing Local ecosystem.
    *   Integrate rich "Beli-like" saving/list features combined with the universal **Rolodex** capability, allowing users to save and semantically search *everything* across the internet (TikToks, IG Reels, Articles, Maps).
2.  **Phase 2: The Action-Oriented AI Executive Assistant:** 
    *   Leverage the trusted Beli/Rolodex/Search ecosystem to expand beyond just finding restaurants/bars.
    *   Deploy the AI Assistant to complete complex, **real-world tasks**.
    *   **Expansion targets:** Scheduling mechanics, booking doctors/dentists, arranging boat tours, managing hotel confirmations, etc.
    *   *Monetization mechanism:* This expanded utility and seamless execution of high-friction real-world tasks is the primary vehicle to drive monetization (e.g., pro-tier subscriptions) and achieve the 1k MRR milestone.
